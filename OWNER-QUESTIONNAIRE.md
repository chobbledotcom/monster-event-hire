# Owner Questionnaire - Joanne & David

A working document for the owners. Every question below is one we cannot resolve by reading the existing site, the EEAT brief or the testimonials. The answers will feed directly into rewrites of the About page, the homepage trust strip, the inspections page, the area pages and the product pages, and will fill obvious holes in EEAT (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) and searcher intent.

**How to use this**: don't try to fill it all in one go. Each section stands alone. Bullet answers, half-sentences, "see attached photo" are all fine. Where a question feels wrong or off-base, please correct it rather than skipping it. The phrasing on the site is ours to fix; the facts have to come from you.

A handful of questions are flagged **CONTRADICTION** - those are places where two pages on the site currently say different things and we need to know which is right.

---

## 1. Identity, history and the trading name

The site uses two names for the business, with no public explanation of the relationship between them.

### 1.1 The two names

The customer-facing brand is **Monster Event Hire**. Every Terms & Conditions clause, by contrast, names **Little Monsters Event Hire** as the contracting party (`terms-conditions/index.html:55, :60, :88, :92`). The header of the T&Cs document is `Little Monsters Event Hire CONTRACT TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF HIRE`.

- Which is the current legal/trading name on the VAT certificate and at Companies House?
- Was there a deliberate rebrand from Little Monsters to Monster Event Hire, and roughly when? Or are both names still in active use depending on context (e.g. legal vs. marketing)?
- Why the change, if it was one? Was Little Monsters originally a children's-party-only brand that grew into corporate work, or was it just shorter and easier to say?
- Do you still answer the phone as Little Monsters if someone uses that name?

### 1.2 The founding story (for the About page rewrite)

The site says only that the business was "established since 2002 by Joanne and David Morris". That is a fact, not a story, and Google's helpful-content guidance rewards the story.

- How did you start in 2002? One castle and a van, or had you been doing this through someone else's company first?
- What were you both doing before 2002?
- Who came up with the name "Little Monsters"? And later, what prompted dropping the "Little"?
- Was 2002 a side project that grew, or did you both leave employment to go in full-time?
- Did the business ever come close to closing - recession, COVID, a year of bad weather - and what kept it going? (Useful for the rewrite, not for verbatim publication.)
- Is there a photo from the early years (a van, a first castle, a market stall) we can use on the About page?

### 1.3 Family involvement

There is a 2017 testimonial from a **Claire Morris** (`reviews/2017-08-13-11-claire-morris.md`). Same family?

- Is Claire a relative? Have any of your children worked in the business?
- Are any of the named "Joanne" / "David" mentions in reviews actually you turning up to a job personally, or has the day-to-day delivery always been done by employees?
- Is the business set up to continue if one of you stepped back? (This matters for trust signals around longevity.)

## 4. The 24 years - facts we can actually publish

The 2002 founding date gives us the strongest single experience claim on the site, but it's currently a single sentence. A few specifics would let us tell the story properly.

### 4.1 Volume

These don't have to be precise - rough orders of magnitude land much better than a vague "many".

- Roughly how many events have you delivered, lifetime? (Even "~30 a week in peak season, ~24 years" gets us into a useful range.)
- Roughly how many distinct customers have you served? How much of the business in any given year is repeat custom vs. new?
- How many items in the current hire fleet? The EEAT brief uses "200+" - is that still right, or has the fleet grown / shrunk? Any approximate split between inflatables, games, electronics, marquees?

### 4.2 The repeat clients

The 2017 testimonials name a few. We want to know which of these are still happening in 2026, and which others we haven't heard about.

- **East Hampshire District Council** - Lucy Soal's 2017 testimonial said "5 years and counting". Is that still going? How many consecutive years now?
- **STFC at Cosener's House** (Abingdon) - one-off in 2017 or repeat?
- **CooperVision** (contact lens manufacturer, Hampshire) - one-off or recurring corporate?
- **Ealing Playday** - still annual?
- **Havant Family Church** - still a client?
- Are there other long-running annual fixtures we should know about? Any major university summer balls, school summer fairs, council fun days that have run for 5+ years?

### 4.3 The standout jobs

- What's the biggest event you've ever delivered (most kit, longest setup, most staff on site)?
- What's the furthest-from-Havant job you've done, and what made it worth the travel?

### 4.4 The fleet over time

- Have you bought any major new pieces in the last 2-3 years? Anything we should be putting on the homepage as "new for [year]"?

## 7. The questions a customer types into Google but the site doesn't answer

Searcher intent: people land on the site with a specific question in mind. We currently answer almost none of these in body copy. Quick owner answers will let us add a proper FAQ.

### 7.1 Booking and lead time

- How far in advance should someone book for: a weekend in May? A bank holiday Saturday? An August Saturday? A Christmas party in early December?
- What's the latest you'd realistically take a booking? "Same day" or "next morning" if you happen to be free?
- Do you keep a waiting list / cancellation list, and is that worth a customer asking about?
- What's a typical lead time for council and corporate work vs. private parties (these are usually wildly different)?

### 7.2 Pricing - the elephant in the room

The site explains why prices aren't published (`pricing-information/index.html:49`, `about-us/index.html:56`), but customers still need a ballpark before they'll send an enquiry.

- Is there a **minimum hire fee** (e.g. you don't take a job under £X all-in)? Knowing this saves both sides time.
- For a typical kids' bouncy castle hire to a Havant back garden, one day, what does that usually come to (a range is fine)? Same question for: a wedding marquee, a rodeo bull with operator, a corporate fun day with three inflatables and two staff.
- Is the rate fundamentally a per-item + per-hour-of-staff + travel calculation, or does duration not affect price much once you're set up?
- Do you ever publish package prices to schools and councils ("school fun day from £X")? We could promote those without revealing private-party rates.

### 7.3 Setup, power, and site requirements

The Terms cover some of this in legal language (clause 1.E, 1.F, 1.G, 1.pp). It needs to be on the product pages and the FAQ in plain English.

- What's a typical setup time for: a single bouncy castle, a 50ft assault course, a marquee, an electronic game like Cash Grabber?
- For inflatables, is one 13-amp socket enough or do you typically need more? Do you ever bring a generator, and is that an extra charge?
- What's the no-grass policy? Terms clause 1.pp says inflatables go on grass only unless indoors. Do you ever set up on hardstanding with mats? On decking? In a flat without garden access?
- Vehicle access - what's the narrowest gate you can get through? Any postcodes where you've had to turn around (so we can warn customers)?
- The 50-metre vehicle-to-site rule (clause 1.F) - is that strict or pragmatic in practice?

### 7.4 Weather and cancellation

Terms cover wind. Rain and other weather conditions are mostly silent.

- Wet weather: do you cancel, postpone, or proceed? Who decides - you, or the customer?
- If it's a marquee booking and the day is washed out, what happens to deposits?
- Have you had to evacuate an event mid-day due to wind in recent seasons? Useful as a "we actually use the anemometer protocol" story.
- The cancellation clause (1.K) is staged at 25% / 50% / 75% / 100% from booking to event date. Is that still current? Is the "unless a government lockdown" clause now obsolete, or is it deliberately kept in?

### 7.5 Permits, councils and licensed premises

- Do you ever apply for street-party permits, road closures, or park-use permits on the customer's behalf? Or is that always the customer's job?
- For events on council land, do you usually liaise direct with the council's events officer, or only via the customer?
- For pub / club / licensed-premises hires, what's the actual process? Do you ask for the venue's licence details? Does the additional licensed-premises insurance need to be requested or is it on every job?
- Any events you've turned down on safety / legal grounds? "We don't operate at unlicensed raves" - that kind of thing is worth saying.

### 7.6 The bits the FAQ never thinks of

- Do you supply tables, chairs and PA / power generally, or only the kit on the product pages?
- Can a customer hire a single piece for a multi-day event (Friday to Sunday) at a discounted rate, or is each day a separate hire?
- Do you ever sell second-hand kit when retiring it? Worth a "fleet refresh" page if yes.
- Are you VAT-inclusive or VAT-exclusive in customer-facing prices? Schools and charities sometimes ask whether you can zero-rate.
- Do you do indoor-only school PE-hall hires in winter? (Helps lift the off-season pages.)

## 8. Geographic reality vs. site claims

The site has dedicated landing pages for 41 areas, covering everywhere from Havant to Kensington and Oxford. The honest question is which of those areas you actually want to win business in.

- Of the 41 areas, which are core (you deliver to weekly in season), which are extended reach (you'd take a job there, no problem), and which are aspirational (a single past job, or none yet)?
- Are there any postcodes you would now decline as too far for the margin?
- The PIPA page mentions Welsh clients. Where in Wales, roughly? Are these inspection or hire customers?
- Anywhere in Hampshire / Surrey / Sussex / London / Oxon where you'd actively like more business that doesn't have an area page yet? (Easier to spin up new pages than fight to rank for ones with no real local activity.)
- Anywhere you've decided you no longer want to serve (and would like the corresponding area page quietly removed)?

## 9. Final catch-all

- What do customers most commonly get wrong when they enquire? (e.g. "people always think a 50ft assault course will fit in a normal garden".) This is gold for product-page copy.
- What do other hire companies in the area do that you wouldn't, and why? (We won't name competitors on the site, but the answer often crystallises the brand's actual position.)
- What's the single thing about the business that you wish customers understood before they enquire? If we had to put one sentence above the homepage hero, what would you want it to say?
- Anything on the current site you actively dislike, that we haven't already flagged?

## 10. How we'll use these answers

Every answer feeds at least one of the following downstream tasks:

- **About page rewrite** - founding story, staff names, family, the 24-year arc, the inspection side, headcount and family.
- **Homepage trust strip and hero rewrite** - the single best sentence about the business; named clients; volume figures; geographic reality.
- **`/inflatable-testing-and-pipa-inspections/` rewrite** - to replace the AI-generated copy with a real account of the inspections side, MOD/schools/leisure customers, training, the Welsh clients.
- **Area pages (per `LOCATION-PAGES-AUDIT.md`)** - local-anchor sentences ("we're 18 miles down the A3"), area-specific testimonials, real coverage tiers.
- **New FAQ page or product-page additions** - lead time, minimum hire fee, ballpark pricing, setup time, power requirements, weather policy.
- **Schema.org markup fixes** - real address, phone, opening hours and (where relevant) social profiles in the homepage Organization block.
- **T&Cs reconciliation** - aligning the legal entity name and the customer-facing brand.

You won't see any of these answers go on the public site verbatim. Everything you write here gets rewritten in the house voice (per `VOICE.md`) before it's published, and run past you for sign-off.
